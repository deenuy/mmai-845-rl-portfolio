# Presentation Notes

## Audience Assumption

This presentation is aimed at classmates who understand RL, state/action/reward design, benchmarking, and model validation, but may know nothing about the detailed history of this project.

## Recommended Speaking Strategy

- Spend little time on raw code.
- Spend more time on:
  - environment assumptions
  - why specific modelling decisions were made
  - what failed
  - how we validated the strategy
  - how the research became a product shell

## Important Framing

Do not oversell the project as finished production trading software.

A stronger framing is:

- reproducible RL research pipeline
- validated portfolio environment
- strong benchmarking process
- first usable product shell for human review and execution

## Where To Be Careful

- Slide 1 uses an illustrative long-run fee-drag chart. Present it as a motivation graphic, not as a literal project backtest since 1975.
- Tory is a personalization example, not the default mainline.
- Phase 4 productization is local-first and not yet broker-integrated.
